MILKHA SINGH

Introduction

Milkha Singh (20 November 1929 â€“ 18 June 2021), also known as "The Flying Sikh", was an Indian track and field sprinter who was introduced to the sport while serving in the Indian Army. He is the only athlete to win gold at 400 metres at the Asian Games as well as the Commonwealth Games. He also won gold medals in the 1958 and 1962 Asian Games.Milkha Singh set a national record in the 1960 Olympics by winning fourth place in the 400m race in 45.73 seconds. This record remained for almost 40 years. He represented India in the 1956 Summer Olympics in Melbourne, the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome and the 1964 Summer Olympics in Tokyo. He was awarded the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian honour, in recognition of his sporting achievements.

Early Life

Milkha Singh was born in 1929 in Gobindpura village in the Muzaffargarh district of Pakistan. He spent most of his childhood in poverty. He lost his 14 siblings to poverty, poor health and lack of medical attention. During the 1947 partition, he became an orphan. His parents, a brother and two sisters were killed in the violence that ensued between the villagers and Muslims who tried to convert them. He witnessed these killings and after that he moved to India. He earned his living by working in a roadside restaurant before joining the army.Singh became disenchanted with his life and considered becoming a criminal but was instead persuaded by one of his brothers, Malkhan, to attempt recruitment to the Indian Army. He successfully gained entrance on his fourth attempt, in 1951. During his military training, he realised his sprinting abilities. Hawaldar Gurudev Singh in 1951 persuaded him to run a cross-country race, as a part of military training.

Career

Milkha singh came into limelight during the 1956 National Games. In the 1958 National Games held at Cuttack, he broke the record in 200m and 400m race.On May 28, 1958, Milkha Singh won India its first gold medal in a track and field event, sprinting through 400 meters at the Asian Games in Tokyo in 47 seconds flat. The next day, he turned it into a double triumph, finishing the 200-meter race in 21.6 seconds for another gold.Singhâ€™s career was shaped by a number of coaches. Among them were Gurdev Singh, who trained him in the army, and Baldev Singh, who mentored him in the national camps. Ranbir Singh and Dronacharya awardee J S Saini coached him too. But his most chronicled coach is Arthur W Howard.In the 1962 Asian Games, Milkha Singh won 2 gold medals, onein 400m and the other in 4 x 100m relay.(TIMESOFINDIA.COM / Updated: Sep 8)

Later Life

Milkha Singh was awarded the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian award, following his success in 1958.
